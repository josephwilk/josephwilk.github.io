Accessibility Aids 2022

Brighton, UK: Draw to Performance festival 2022.

A live performance working with various forms of Accessibility tools: Digital pen, Programming code, Robot pen-plotter & Paint.

All of these tools used have no feedback or sensors of the mediums they are working with.They are all drawing tools with various levels of feedback that are used as assisitive tools acting as different extensions of the body. Assistive technology is complex and in drawing with it its about not trying to erasing the disabiled identify by enabling drawing as an able-bodies person. These tools have a resistance in how they are design to be used or how they are seen. For example a digital pen is not a pen, so is it really drawing? These failings and constrasts between machine bodies and human bodies, are present within the work.

The work begins with a hand draw line which acts as the input for all of the performance. Working interactively with programming code and exploring the parameter space the line is turned into a pattern. Paint is carefully placed by hand on a canvas and finally the penplotter using various clay sculpting tools maniplates the paint.

There are indirect forms or accessiblity aid present in the performance in the form of wheelchair and glasses. All of the tools provides some form of assistance in the process. The context of how these tools are seen can range from the medical, creative, futuristic & fashion but they are all accessibility aids.

The performance draws from the bluring line between bodies and machines that comes from the experience of living with a wheelchair as a body.
