Average Lords

The House of Lords is one of the largest legislative assemblies in the world. Appointment is through Prime ministers nomination or nomination (from yourself or somone else) to the House of Lords appointment commision.

Applications are open all year round: Apply now!

There are roughly 2 appointments per year from applying directly to the House of Lords appointment commision. So best bet is being nominated by an out-going Prime minister as they can pick as many and who they want and nobody can do anything about it. Even if the security services, MPs or the House of Lords appointment commision are unhappy its all good.

The House of Lords Act 1999 set out to remove membership of the House of Lords by virtue of a hereditary peerage. The act however featured an amendment that allowed 92 hereditary peers to remain members.

So in 2022 there are 92 Hereditary peers sitting in the house of Lords. All 808 Hereditary peers in the UK are entitled to sit in the house of Lords but limited to those 92 seats. If a peer dies they are replaced through a vote, usually by the other hereditary peers sitting in the house of lords of the same party. In this way the party of Hereditary peers seats are fixed as 42 Conservative, 2 Labour, 42 Lib Dems & 28 Cross Bench.

This project takes all profile images of the current members of the House of Lords and averages their pixels together, creating a combined image representing many different peers. It draws out the dominant appearances of the House of Lords.
