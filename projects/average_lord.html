Average Lords

The House of Lords is one of the largest legislative assemblies in the world. Appointment is through Prime ministers nomination or nomination (from yourself or somone else) to the House of Lords appointment commision.

Applications are open all year round: Apply now!

There are only roughly 2 appointments per year from applying directly to the House of Lords appointment commision. So best bet is being nominated by an out-going Prime minister as they can pick as many and who they want and nobody can do anything about it. Even if the security services, MPs or the House of Lords appointment commision are unhappy its all good.

In 2022 there are 92 Hereditary peers, children who inhertied their titles from their parents. There are actually 808 Hereditary peers in the UK but due to the Lords reform act only 92 of them are allowed to sit in the House of Lords. If a sitting peer dies another one is voted in from those 808. The remainder of the House of Lords are life time, serving peers. Getting rid a Peer is tricky, the King/Queen does not have the power to cancel a peerage once it has been created. Parlimient can but the precedent was rather specific to the First World War and dealing with spies. There seems to be a hope that if your ended up in the House of Lords you are of a sufficent character that you would resign from it if you commited too many obvious crimes or unethical behaviour. Upon resigning you would still get to keep your title because you earnt it.

This project takes all profile images of the current members of the House of Lords and averages their pixels together, creating a combined image representing many different peers. It draws out the dominant appearances of the House of Lords.
